Historical Background

Japan was very behind in the race to modernization until the Meiji Restoration when Japan spent almost all their moeny to train workers and begin their industrial revoultion. Eventually bankrupting them during WWll. Until they were saved by an economic mericle, America began providing glodal support to forien countries. During this time of recovery Japan hit a annual GDP growth exceeding 10% in some decades. AS all good things must come to an end the new found wealth throughout Japan was used recklessly leading to a major economic collapse from 1990 to 1992 Equity values fell around 60% and land values dropped around 70%. GDP dropped from $5.55 trillion to around $4.27 trillion. This collaspe although slowed has lasted 30 years into the present day.
02

The Economy Today (2026)

Today Japan's economic stagnation seems to be comming to an end as the economy growes back at an annual rate of 2.1%. This economic resurection seems to be stemming from strong cunsumer spending and exporting. Althought the most significant change was in inflation and wage growth. So, after years of a zero growth economy Japan has finnally began to push through the stagnate economy.
03

Outlook & Key Risks

As of now the Economy of Japan seems to be comming of of it's dangerous stagnation and growth will likely slow to a stable level of growth until a new equalibrium is reached with higher prices and higher wages.
Although Japan has started towrads its good fortune several risks may still exist. Including the raising crude oil prices in the Middle East. But more dangerous is the high debt-to-GDP ration being one of the highest in the world nearly 2 times the ratio of the United States. The Shocking Ratio sits at roughly 245% dept-to-GDP.
